## Pros and cons

The physician-only prescribing model stands out. Plenty of telehealth platforms rely on nurse practitioners or PAs, which is legally fine but not the same clinical depth that some patients want from a hormone specialist. Nexel also covers all 50 states and ships medication to patients' doors, so geography isn't a limiting factor beyond the age floor. The variety of treatment options is above average, and insurance acceptance opens a door that most competitors keep shut.

On the downside, the pricing opacity is a real friction point. "Typically under $100 per month" is encouraging, but it's not a quote, and there's no public schedule of fees, consultation costs, or what happens if labs reveal something that requires a more involved protocol. The 30-and-older restriction is firmer than at many competitors, which cap eligibility lower or don't specify one at all. Labs being entirely outside the program adds a step that some patients - especially those new to TRT - may not anticipate. And while physical offices in CA and FL are a plus for patients in those states, they don't help anyone else.

## Bottom line

Nexel Medical fits a specific patient well: an adult over 30 who wants a physician-run hormone practice, values insurance billing, and is comfortable handling their own lab logistics. The broad treatment menu and nationwide telehealth coverage are genuine strengths, and the sub-$100-per-month indication on testosterone therapy is competitive if it holds in practice. The lack of transparent pricing and bundled labs make it harder to evaluate on paper compared to some competitors. If that sounds like you, the intake process will answer the questions the website leaves open.
