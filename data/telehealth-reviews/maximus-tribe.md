## How Maximus works

Maximus is a telehealth hormone platform built around a specific clinical stance: standard TRT suppresses your body's own testosterone production, and that doesn't have to be the default. Rather than leading with straight testosterone injections, Maximus combines exogenous testosterone with enclomiphene - a selective estrogen receptor modulator that stimulates the pituitary gland to maintain its natural LH and FSH output. The effect is that the testes keep producing testosterone on their own even while you're supplementing. That fertility-preserving approach gives Maximus a clear lane and attracts men who want hormone optimization without fully shutting down their own system - whether they're concerned about long-term fertility, testicular atrophy, or just prefer a more physiologically conservative protocol.

The intake process is fully remote. You complete an online health assessment, Maximus ships an at-home lab kit, and a licensed physician reviews your bloodwork before consulting with you directly. If you're a candidate for treatment, your prescription ships to your door. The physician prescribing model - as opposed to nurse practitioners or PAs running the consult - is a detail some patients care about, particularly for a Schedule III controlled substance like testosterone. Maximus lists injections as a treatment option alongside cream and oral testosterone, though the published pricing tiers are structured around the combination protocols rather than injections specifically. If you want injectables, bring it up during your consultation.

## Pricing

Maximus publishes its pricing upfront, which puts it ahead of competitors who gate quotes behind an intake form. Two tiers are currently available:

- **Enclomiphene + Testosterone Cream** - $189.99 per month. Includes topical testosterone, enclomiphene, and tadalafil.
- **Enclomiphene + Oral Testosterone** - $199.99 per month. Swaps the cream for oral testosterone, with enclomiphene and tadalafil included.

The $10 spread between tiers is minor. The more notable detail is that tadalafil - typically prescribed for erectile dysfunction and sometimes used to support vascular health more broadly - is bundled into both plans at no extra charge. Most telehealth TRT platforms treat tadalafil as a paid add-on; Maximus includes it by default.

Lab costs are where the data is less clear. At-home kits ship to you, but whether lab fees are rolled into the monthly price or billed separately isn't spelled out in the available plan details. Confirm that before you sign up.

## What's included

Both tiers cover prescription testosterone, enclomiphene, free tadalafil, at-home lab testing, and ongoing physician supervision. The enclomiphene isn't an optional upgrade - it's built into the protocol by design. Maximus describes its combination approach as patented, developed with input from academic urologists who have published research in this area. That clinical pedigree is a genuine differentiator from the crop of TRT-only platforms staffed primarily by functional medicine practitioners.

Medications ship directly to your address. State availability isn't listed publicly, which means you'll find out whether you're covered during intake rather than beforehand.

## Pros and cons

The fertility-friendly framing isn't just marketing positioning - it reflects a real clinical philosophy, and the research background of the medical team gives it credibility. Bundling enclomiphene as a baseline component rather than an expensive add-on means you're getting a more complete protocol at a price that holds up reasonably well against competitors. The included tadalafil is a straightforward perk. Transparent pricing is rarer than it should be in this category, and Maximus earns credit for publishing it.

The downsides are worth noting. Maximus has expanded significantly into weight loss programs and peptide therapies, making TRT one product line among several rather than the company's core focus. That's not a disqualifier, but it shapes where institutional attention goes. The bigger practical concern is that specific state availability isn't disclosed - you won't know whether Maximus operates in your state until you're partway through intake. For a controlled substance, state-by-state prescribing rules matter, and the lack of upfront clarity on this is a friction point.

## Bottom line

Maximus is worth considering if you want a fertility-aware TRT protocol led by physicians with genuine research credentials, and you'd rather not negotiate enclomiphene as a separate prescription from a separate provider. The transparent pricing, included tadalafil, and combination-first clinical approach make it easier to evaluate than most competitors. Confirm lab fees and your state's eligibility before committing.
