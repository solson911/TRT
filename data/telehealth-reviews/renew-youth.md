## How Renew Youth Works

Renew Youth has been in the hormone health space since 1999, which puts it well ahead of the wave of telehealth TRT startups that launched in the 2010s and 2020s. That longevity counts for something - the company has had 25-plus years to refine its clinical approach and figure out what patients actually need. Their focus extends beyond testosterone alone into broader hormone optimization, which may appeal to men whose bloodwork shows issues beyond low T. The model is physician-led, with synchronous video consultations, so you're meeting with a real doctor rather than filling out a form and waiting for a prescription to appear.

The intake process starts with a free consultation - no purchase required - where you discuss symptoms and goals. From there, if you move forward, labs are ordered to establish your baseline. Renew Youth ships medications directly to your home, so there's no pharmacy run involved. The consultation model uses live appointments rather than asynchronous messaging, which tends to mean more back-and-forth with your care team but also requires scheduling.

## Pricing

Renew Youth's published starting price is $218 per month for their Inclusive Plan. That price covers lab testing every six months, physician oversight, home delivery of medications, and access to a health consultation team. A single monthly figure is all the company discloses publicly, so if your treatment protocol runs outside the standard configuration, you'll need to get a specific quote. Insurance isn't accepted, and it's unclear whether FSA or HSA funds can be applied - worth asking during the free consultation if that matters to you.

At $218 monthly, Renew Youth sits in the mid-to-upper range compared to newer TRT telehealth entrants, some of which advertise starting prices closer to $100-150. The trade-off is a more established clinical infrastructure and a broader hormone service menu.

## What's Included

The Inclusive Plan bundles quite a bit into one price. Lab work is included every six months - not at-home finger-prick testing, but standard labs through a draw site. Medications ship directly to you and cover the main delivery options: injection, oral, and topical. You're not locked into a single format, which matters since patient response to different delivery methods varies. Physician oversight is ongoing, and you have access to a health consultation team between appointments.

The six-month lab cadence is worth noting. Some TRT providers test quarterly, especially during the first year when dialing in a protocol requires closer monitoring. If your physician wants more frequent checks, it's worth clarifying whether that's available and whether it affects pricing.

## Pros and Cons

Renew Youth's age is its clearest advantage. Twenty-five years in this space means the clinical protocols, patient workflows, and regulatory relationships are mature. For men who are skeptical of newer telehealth TRT companies with thin track records, that history provides some reassurance. The free initial consultation is also a low-pressure entry point - you can ask hard questions before committing to anything.

The scope of treatment beyond testosterone is another genuine differentiator. If your evaluation suggests that low T is part of a broader hormonal picture, Renew Youth is positioned to address that without sending you to a separate provider.

On the downside, the pricing structure is opaque beyond the $218 starting figure. If your protocol needs anything outside the standard, you won't know the cost until you're already in the process. Insurance is not accepted, period, so the full monthly cost lands on you. Geographic coverage isn't universal either - virtual appointments are available in most states but not all, and the company doesn't publish a specific list of covered states, so you'll need to confirm your state is included before getting too far into intake.

The at-home medication delivery works well for injections and topicals, but the lab draw requirement means you're still making a trip somewhere every six months - it's not a fully at-home experience.

## Bottom Line

Renew Youth suits men who want a clinically established provider with a long track record and a broader hormone-health scope than pure-play TRT services offer. The $218 monthly all-in price is competitive if you account for included labs and physician oversight, though the lack of pricing transparency beyond the starting rate is a real frustration. If you're in a covered state and prefer a synchronous, physician-led model over the asynchronous intake common at newer TRT platforms, it's worth the free consultation.
