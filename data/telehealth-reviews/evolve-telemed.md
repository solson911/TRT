## Pricing

Evolve's pricing starts at $75 per month, but what sits above that floor isn't clearly spelled out on the public site. You'll likely need to complete an intake and consultation before getting a precise quote for your protocol.

Insurance is not accepted. This is a cash-pay practice, which means you're covering costs directly out of pocket. On the upside, FSA and HSA cards are accepted - a practical benefit if you've been setting aside pre-tax dollars and want somewhere to use them. Lab costs at Labcorp are billed separately and vary depending on your coverage and the panels ordered, so build that into your budget estimate before getting started.

## What's included

Treatment is built around comprehensive lab work and customized protocols. Injectable testosterone and oral testosterone are both available, which covers the two delivery methods most men are asking about. Medications ship to your door; you won't need to manage a pharmacy pickup.

Ongoing care includes access to your dedicated care coordinator and physician follow-ups for monitoring and dose adjustments. The in-person consultation option adds something most telehealth TRT services don't offer, and it's available alongside standard video visits.

## Pros and cons

The strongest argument for Evolve is clinical rigor. Physicians who specialize in hormone therapy are better equipped to manage the nuances - adjusting for hematocrit, dialing in a dose that works without overreaching - than a generalist fitting TRT into a broader primary care practice. Comprehensive lab work as the foundation of your protocol, rather than a symptom questionnaire alone, reflects a more thorough intake process.

The care coordinator model adds real value for men who've been burned by providers that are hard to reach. Having a specific person to contact cuts down on the frustration of chasing responses through a generic inbox.

The drawbacks are practical rather than fundamental. No insurance means this is an out-of-pocket expense for most men; the $75/month floor is accessible, but the full monthly cost - labs, any add-ons, follow-up visits - could run higher once your protocol is established. The 10-business-day shipping window is worth flagging for anyone starting fresh or worried about gaps in supply. And if getting to a Labcorp is a real inconvenience where you live, that's a legitimate friction point given that Evolve doesn't offer at-home draws. State availability isn't publicly listed either, so it's worth confirming coverage before investing time in the intake.

## Bottom line

Evolve Telemedicine is a reasonable fit for men who want a more careful, physician-led experience and can handle the out-of-pocket cost. The specialist model, care coordinator support, and flexible consult options add up to something closer to a private practice than a high-volume TRT pipeline. If you're shopping primarily on price, you can find cheaper. If you want a provider that takes your bloodwork seriously and stays accessible between appointments, Evolve is worth considering.
