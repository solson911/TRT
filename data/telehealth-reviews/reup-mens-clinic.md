## What's included

Labs are included, which is a meaningful inclusion at a clinic that doesn't disclose pricing - bundled labs mean you're not getting hit with a separate cost for the bloodwork required to establish and maintain your protocol. The specifics of what the panel covers and how often retesting happens aren't detailed publicly, so those are questions worth raising during the initial consultation.

The treatment menu is injections only. ReUP frames this as a deliberate choice, and there's logic behind it. Injectable testosterone gives your provider precise control over dosage adjustments in a way that pellets - which are implanted and release at a fixed rate until they're absorbed - don't allow. If your levels run high or you have a reaction, a pellet protocol is much harder to walk back quickly. Injections are adjustable. The cross-contamination concern with topical treatments is also a real consideration for men who live with children or partners, and injections eliminate it entirely. None of this means injections are objectively better for every patient, but if you already know you want an injectable protocol, ReUP's focused approach isn't a limitation.

ED treatment is also listed as a service alongside TRT, consistent with the tagline. The details of how that's structured and what medications are involved aren't spelled out publicly.

## Pros and cons

The strongest arguments for ReUP are the synchronous consult model and the injection-specific focus. A live appointment means you can ask questions and get answers in real time rather than waiting for a message response - that matters when you're evaluating a new treatment. The free initial testosterone check is a genuine upside; it's a low-friction way to find out where your levels are before you've agreed to anything. Texas coverage via telehealth means you're not driving to a clinic if you're somewhere without convenient in-person options.

The limitations start with geography - Texas only, full stop. If you move or travel frequently, coverage becomes an issue. Insurance isn't accepted and the services are characterized as investigational, which is language that often signals you'll be paying the full cost out of pocket with no path to reimbursement through your health plan. The absence of public pricing means you're going in without a clear cost picture, which is a real inconvenience. And with injections as the only delivery format, men who want a non-injectable option - whether because of needle aversion, lifestyle, or a physician recommendation - won't find it here.

## Bottom line

ReUP Men's Clinic is a narrow fit for a specific kind of patient: a Texas resident who wants injectable TRT managed via telehealth, with a real consultation rather than an async questionnaire flow. If that description fits you, the free initial check, included labs, and synchronous consult model make it a reasonable place to start. If you need multi-state coverage, a non-injectable delivery format, or a clinic that publishes pricing upfront, you'll want to look elsewhere.

Testosterone is a Schedule III controlled substance - any provider needs to evaluate you properly before prescribing, and ReUP's sync consult model at least checks that box.
