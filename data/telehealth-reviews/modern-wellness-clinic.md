## Pros and cons

The integrated care model is the clearest advantage here. Labs, physician oversight, and treatment delivery all happen through the same practice rather than being stitched together across separate providers. Telehealth access across Nevada means you don't have to live near Summerlin to get started, though you would need to travel there for any in-person visits.

Insurance acceptance is a genuine differentiator. Most telehealth TRT platforms operate on a cash-pay model, so if you have coverage that might apply to hormone therapy, this is one of the few places where that conversation is even possible.

The downsides are also real. Pricing opacity is a friction point - without a baseline number, it's hard to know whether to invest the time in an intake. The Summerlin clinic is the only physical location in Nevada, so anyone outside the Las Vegas metro is limited to telehealth. And the broader wellness focus cuts both ways: the clinic has range, but TRT isn't its core identity, which means the depth of hormone-specific expertise may not match what you'd find at a dedicated men's health provider.

If you want a specialist who eats and breathes testosterone protocols, the generalist model may feel like a mismatch. If you want a medical home where TRT is one piece of a larger care picture - and where insurance has a shot at covering some of it - the trade-off may be worth it.
