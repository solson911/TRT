## What's included

Labs are included and follow-ups happen every three months - a quarterly rhythm that keeps closer tabs on your levels than the semi-annual schedule some competitors use. Comprehensive bloodwork paired with 90-day retesting is a reasonable baseline for managing TRT safely, and having it bundled rather than billed separately simplifies the cost picture.

REGENX prescribes across four delivery formats: injections, topical cream, oral testosterone, and nasal gel. That's a wider menu than most telehealth clinics offer. Nasal gel in particular is less commonly available - it's a solid option for men who want to avoid injections and creams, or who have concerns about transference with topical formulas. Having all four available means the prescribing physician can fit the method to your situation rather than defaulting to whatever the clinic stocks.

The prescriber model is physician-only, and specifically board-certified urologists with 15 or more years of experience. Urology is one of the most directly relevant specialties for TRT - urologists deal with testosterone, reproductive health, and related endocrine issues as a core part of their training, not an add-on. That credential is more specific and arguably more relevant than the generic "board-certified physician" language a lot of telehealth TRT platforms use.
