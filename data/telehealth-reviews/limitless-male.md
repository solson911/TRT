## How Limitless Male works

Limitless Male is a brick-and-mortar men's health clinic network founded in 2016, with 16 locations spread across eight Midwest states: Iowa, Kansas, Nebraska, North Dakota, South Dakota, Michigan, Montana, and Illinois. Before you read further, one thing is worth saying plainly - this is not a telehealth service. Visits are in person, at one of their physical clinics. If you live outside their footprint or prefer the remote model, Limitless Male is not the right fit. For men in the Midwest who want to sit across from a clinician and handle everything locally, it may be exactly what they're looking for.

The intake process begins with an in-clinic consultation. A provider reviews your symptoms and health history in a synchronous, face-to-face appointment - not a video call or async questionnaire. Lab work is drawn on-site, so there's no third-party lab order to sort out yourself. Once results are back, the care team builds a personalized treatment plan. Follow-up monitoring and lab draws happen at the same clinic, keeping the whole care loop under one roof.

## Pricing

Public pricing is not disclosed on the Limitless Male website. You'll need to contact a clinic or book a consultation to get a quote. The company does advertise that insurance is not required and that pricing is "transparent," which suggests flat-rate or membership-style billing - but the actual numbers aren't available without going through intake. FSA and HSA eligibility isn't confirmed in their public materials either. If cost is a deciding factor, plan on a phone call before you commit to anything.

## What's included

Limitless Male includes comprehensive bloodwork as part of the program - drawn in-clinic, not shipped to your door. Treatment options span injectable testosterone, oral formulations, and peptides. The oral option is notable; it's less common in TRT programs and may suit men who are uncomfortable with self-injection. Peptide therapy is listed as an available add-on, which is common at clinics that position themselves as broader "men's performance" practices rather than narrow hormone replacement providers.

Because labs are handled in-clinic rather than through a home kit, patients don't need to manage specimen shipping or schedule a separate draw at a LabCorp or Quest location. For some men that's a convenience; for others, it's a constraint that ties them to clinic hours and geography.

One caveat worth noting: compounded medications are listed among their offerings. Compounded drugs are not FDA-approved as finished products - they're mixed to order by a compounding pharmacy. That's common practice in TRT, and not necessarily a red flag, but it's something to understand before starting a protocol.

## Pros and cons

The strongest argument for Limitless Male is scale and reputation within their region. Over 100,000 patients and a 4.9 Google rating across their locations is a meaningful signal - that kind of consistency is hard to fake across 16 clinics. Men in the Midwest who want in-person care, real lab draws, and a clinician they can look in the eye will find a well-reviewed, established option here.

The in-person model also means your protocol is managed by providers who can observe you directly, rather than interpreting your symptoms through a video screen. For men who are new to TRT or have complex health histories, that hands-on continuity can matter.

The main limitation is geographic. Eight states is a real footprint for a regional chain, but it's eight states. If you live in Ohio, Missouri, or anywhere outside that corridor, the clinic is simply not accessible. And even within covered states, 16 locations spread across a large geographic area means your nearest clinic might still be a meaningful drive.

The requirement for in-person visits also runs against the convenience argument that draws many men to the telehealth TRT model in the first place. No remote consultations, no medications shipped to your door - you're working around clinic hours and physical proximity. That's a feature for some patients and a dealbreaker for others.

Undisclosed public pricing makes it harder to comparison-shop. "Transparent pricing" is mentioned in their marketing, but without published numbers, prospective patients are taking that claim on faith until they call in.

## Bottom line

Limitless Male is a regional in-person clinic network, not a telehealth provider, and it should be evaluated on those terms. For men in Iowa, Kansas, Nebraska, the Dakotas, Michigan, Montana, or Illinois who want face-to-face TRT care from a clinic with a strong local track record, it's a well-established option with a loyal patient base. For anyone outside that region, or anyone who needs the flexibility of remote care, look elsewhere. Call before committing - pricing isn't public and the in-person requirement needs to work for your schedule and location.
