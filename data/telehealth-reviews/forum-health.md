## How Forum Health works

Forum Health is a network of integrative medicine practices operating across 13 states, with telehealth access built on top of that brick-and-mortar foundation. It is not a TRT-only company - testosterone therapy is one service among many that includes thyroid management, autoimmune support, metabolic health, and chronic disease care. If you want a clinic that treats hormone optimization as one piece of a broader health picture, Forum Health is worth a look. If you want a bare-bones, testosterone-focused subscription, you will likely find it over-built for your needs.

The intake process starts with a synchronous consultation - a live video or phone appointment with a physician, not an asynchronous questionnaire. Forum Health fields more than 80 board-certified doctors and specialists across its network, and the model leans toward root-cause diagnostics rather than treating a number on a lab panel. That means appointments tend to run longer and more conversational than what you get from a high-volume TRT telehealth platform.

Treatment options include injectable testosterone, pellet therapy, and oral formulations. The prescriber model is mixed, meaning some patients are seen by MDs and others by nurse practitioners or physician assistants depending on location and availability. Medications are prescribed through the platform, though Forum Health's site does not publish details about pharmacy partners or shipping logistics.

## Pricing

Public pricing is not disclosed anywhere on Forum Health's website. You will not find a monthly rate, a lab fee schedule, or a membership tier until you complete an intake consultation and receive a personalized quote. Given the integrative medicine positioning and the physician-heavy staffing model, expect pricing to be on the higher end relative to subscription-first TRT platforms.

Lab work is not included in whatever plan you end up on - that is an additional out-of-pocket cost. Forum Health does not accept government insurance (Medicare or Medicaid), and it is unclear from public information whether private insurance is accepted for any services. FSA and HSA compatibility is not confirmed on their site.

## What's included

Forum Health's differentiator is the diagnostic depth. Rather than ordering a basic testosterone panel and sending a prescription, the clinical model emphasizes identifying root causes - adrenal function, thyroid status, metabolic markers, and so on - before landing on a treatment protocol. For someone who has bounced around generic TRT providers without getting answers, that approach has real appeal.

The treatment menu is broader than most TRT-only platforms. Pellet therapy in particular is not something many telehealth providers offer, since it requires an in-office procedure. Forum Health's hybrid model - telehealth appointments over a network of physical practices - makes that possible in covered states.

Telehealth availability spans CA, CO, FL, IL, MI, NC, OH, SC, TN, TX, UT, VA, and WI. Patients outside those states cannot be seen.

## Pros and cons

The case for Forum Health comes down to clinical philosophy. If you want a provider who will look at your full hormonal picture - not just hand you a testosterone prescription and call it done - the integrative approach and access to over 80 specialists is genuinely useful. The synchronous consult model means you are talking to someone, not filling out a form and waiting for a message back. Pellet therapy being on the table is a meaningful option for men who prefer a less frequent dosing schedule.

The case against is mostly about fit. Forum Health is not built around TRT. It is an integrative medicine network that happens to offer TRT, and that distinction matters when you are comparing convenience, cost, and focus. Pricing opacity is a real friction point - you cannot comparison-shop without going through intake. Labs not being included adds another variable cost on top of an already undisclosed baseline. And the 13-state footprint, while reasonable, will cut out a significant chunk of the country.

## Bottom line

Forum Health makes the most sense for men who suspect their hormone issues are tangled up with other chronic health problems and want a physician-led, root-cause evaluation rather than a quick script. It is probably not the right pick if you are already diagnosed, know what you want, and are looking for the most streamlined or affordable way to stay on therapy. Get a consultation quote before making any assumptions about cost.
