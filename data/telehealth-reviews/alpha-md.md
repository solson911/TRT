## Pros and Cons

The strongest case for Alpha MD is the pricing transparency combined with the all-in cost structure. You're not going to get hit with a separate pharmacy bill at the end of the month. The free at-home lab kit removes a real barrier for people who don't want to schedule a blood draw somewhere, and the military/veteran discount is a concrete benefit that's easy to verify.

The treatment format variety - injections, cream, and oral - gives patients real options, which is worth something when many telehealth TRT services quietly funnel everyone toward the cheapest delivery method.

On the downside, the required minimum commitment means Alpha MD isn't ideal if you're still deciding whether TRT is right for you and want maximum flexibility. The absence of any clear information about insurance or FSA/HSA isn't unusual for this category of provider, but it's still a gap. And without knowing which states Alpha MD serves, it's hard to evaluate the service if you're outside a major metro area.

## Bottom Line

Alpha MD is a clean, well-priced telehealth TRT option for men who want a fully remote experience without hidden costs. The at-home lab kit, bundled medications, synchronous consultations, and multiple treatment formats make it a reasonably complete package in the $98-$129/month range. The three-month minimum is a meaningful commitment for a service you haven't tried yet, and state availability is opaque - so confirm coverage before you go through intake.
