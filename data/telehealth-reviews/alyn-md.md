## Pros and cons

The lab-first structure is the real selling point here. A $100 entry cost is low enough to get genuine clinical data on your hormone levels without committing to an ongoing subscription before you know whether treatment is warranted. Providers that skip straight to the monthly membership without a clear diagnostic phase are making a tradeoff between speed and rigor - AlynMD leans toward rigor.

The $99 monthly rate includes things that other platforms charge separately for or skip entirely: a health coach alongside physician oversight, and ongoing lab monitoring rather than a one-time draw at intake. The multi-modality treatment options - injections, cream, oral - add flexibility that matters if one delivery method doesn't work well for you.

Serving all 50 states puts AlynMD in a narrower group of telehealth TRT providers that can actually reach patients in rural areas or states with limited local options.

The clearest limitation is the explicit exclusion of performance-enhancement and bodybuilding goals. That's a clinically reasonable position for a physician-led practice - testosterone is a Schedule III controlled substance and prescribing it outside a medical context carries real liability - but it does narrow who the service is designed for. There's also no public information on insurance, FSA, or HSA eligibility, which leaves some financial planning questions open until you contact them directly.
