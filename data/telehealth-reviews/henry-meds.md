## Pros and Cons

The all-inclusive pricing is the clearest advantage: one flat monthly rate covers everything, and no contracts mean you're not stuck if the service doesn't suit you. The mixed consult model - video or async depending on your state - is useful for people who can't easily book a synchronous appointment. Both compounded and FDA-approved medication options are available, which gives some flexibility in treatment format.

The downsides are genuine. State coverage is limited and not transparently listed, so you may not find out you're ineligible until after you've completed intake. California residents face longer wait times for fulfillment. The age cap of 35 to 65 will exclude younger men with clinically low testosterone. And because Henry Meds is a general metabolic health platform - weight loss, hormones, and more - TRT isn't getting the specialized clinical focus you'd find at a company that does testosterone exclusively. The depth of hormonal expertise on the clinical team is harder to assess as a result.

## Bottom Line

Henry Meds delivers transparent, all-in monthly pricing and genuine flexibility with no long-term commitment. For men in their late 30s through mid-60s in a covered state who want a straightforward subscription without surprise lab fees, it's a reasonable option to evaluate. The breadth of the platform is both a feature and a limitation - you're getting a generalist metabolic health service, not a TRT-focused clinic.
