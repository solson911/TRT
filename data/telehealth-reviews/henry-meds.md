## How Henry Meds Works

Henry Meds is a telehealth platform built around weight management and hormone optimization - TRT sits alongside GLP-1 medications and other services rather than being the company's core specialty. If you're a man between 35 and 65 looking for a low-friction, all-inclusive monthly subscription for testosterone therapy, and you don't mind that TRT isn't the main event, Henry Meds is worth a look.

The intake starts with an online health questionnaire. Depending on your state, you'll either schedule a video consultation with a provider or have your case reviewed asynchronously. Labs are included in the monthly price, though the data doesn't specify whether blood draws happen at a local lab or through an at-home kit - worth confirming before you start. Once a provider clears you, medication ships directly to your door.

Henry Meds offers three delivery formats: injectable testosterone, topical cream, and oral KYZATREX, an FDA-approved testosterone undecanoate capsule. The oral option is a newer format that some patients prefer over injections and is increasingly common among telehealth TRT providers.

One thing to know upfront: Henry Meds is not available in every state, and the site doesn't publish a clear coverage list before you go through intake. California residents specifically face extended fulfillment times. Confirm availability for your state before you invest time in the process.

## Pricing

Henry Meds charges $129 per month for compounded testosterone therapy or $179 per month for oral KYZATREX. Both tiers are all-inclusive: provider visits, labs, medication, supplies, and shipping are wrapped into one number. No long-term contracts - you can cancel month-to-month.

The company does not accept insurance. FSA or HSA eligibility isn't confirmed in the available information, so check directly if that matters to your budget.

At $129 a month, Henry Meds lands in the middle of the telehealth TRT market - not the cheapest option out there, but competitive for a genuinely flat rate that doesn't add separate charges for labs or shipping.

## What's Included

Both tiers cover the full stack: initial provider evaluation, ongoing labs, medication, supplies, and shipping. The absence of separate lab billing is a meaningful distinction from competitors that advertise a low medication cost and then charge extra for bloodwork.

Treatment options span injections, topical cream, and oral capsules. The compounded tier most likely refers to compounded testosterone cypionate or enanthate - the standard injectable forms - while the $179 tier is specifically KYZATREX. Henry Meds also treats women for hormone optimization, but eligibility for men's TRT is capped at ages 35 to 65, which is narrower than some platforms that will evaluate younger men with documented hypogonadism.

## Pros and Cons

The all-inclusive pricing is the clearest advantage: one flat monthly rate covers everything, and no contracts mean you're not stuck if the service doesn't suit you. The mixed consult model - video or async depending on your state - is useful for people who can't easily book a synchronous appointment. Both compounded and FDA-approved medication options are available, which gives some flexibility in treatment format.

The downsides are genuine. State coverage is limited and not transparently listed, so you may not find out you're ineligible until after you've completed intake. California residents face longer wait times for fulfillment. The age cap of 35 to 65 will exclude younger men with clinically low testosterone. And because Henry Meds is a general metabolic health platform - weight loss, hormones, and more - TRT isn't getting the specialized clinical focus you'd find at a company that does testosterone exclusively. The depth of hormonal expertise on the clinical team is harder to assess as a result.

## Bottom Line

Henry Meds delivers transparent, all-in monthly pricing and genuine flexibility with no long-term commitment. For men in their late 30s through mid-60s in a covered state who want a straightforward subscription without surprise lab fees, it's a reasonable option to evaluate. The breadth of the platform is both a feature and a limitation - you're getting a generalist metabolic health service, not a TRT-focused clinic.
