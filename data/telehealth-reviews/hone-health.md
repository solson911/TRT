## How Hone Health works

Hone Health is a telehealth platform built around the idea that men's hormone optimization is part of a broader longevity picture - not just testosterone, but the full panel of biomarkers that affect energy, body composition, and long-term health. The service is aimed at men who want a comprehensive lab workup, ongoing physician oversight, and the flexibility to choose how involved they want to be. It's a reasonable fit for someone who wants more than a simple TRT script but doesn't need the hand-holding of a concierge clinic.

You begin with a one-time initial test and consult for $65. That covers a 40-plus biomarker blood draw, a synchronous video consultation with a board-certified physician, and a personalized longevity plan based on your results. Labs are not shipped to your home - you go to a local draw site. If your evaluation supports treatment, your physician can prescribe from there. Prescriptions ship directly to you.

After the initial visit, you pick a membership tier. The two ongoing plans differ primarily in how often you get labs and whether physician consults are bundled. Hone does not accept insurance, but both tiers are FSA and HSA eligible, which can meaningfully reduce out-of-pocket costs if you have those accounts available.

## Pricing

The one-time initial consult is $65. After that, ongoing membership runs either $25 per month (Basic) or $149 per month (Premium).

At $25 per month, the Basic tier includes 40-plus biomarker testing every six months, access to member pricing on medications, and the option to purchase additional consults a la carte. It's a lower-cost entry point, but you're largely self-directed - consults aren't bundled in.

The Premium tier at $149 per month includes everything in Basic, plus bundled telehealth consults, personalized treatment protocols, and retesting every 90 days rather than every six months. If you want a physician actively managing your protocol and quarterly check-ins on your levels, this is the tier designed for that.

One important note across both tiers: medications and supplements are charged separately. So your total monthly spend on Premium, for example, will run higher than $149 once you factor in the cost of testosterone or any other prescribed medications.

State coverage is not publicly disclosed, so you'll need to verify eligibility during the intake process.

## What's included

Hone offers three treatment delivery methods: injections, topical cream, and oral testosterone. Having all three available is less common among telehealth TRT platforms, and it means your physician can match the delivery method to your preference and clinical situation rather than defaulting to one option.

The 40-plus biomarker panel is a genuine differentiator. Most basic TRT programs check total testosterone, maybe free testosterone, and a few related markers. Hone's stated panel goes well beyond that, covering the kind of metabolic, thyroid, and cardiovascular markers that give a fuller picture of what's driving symptoms. Retesting frequency depends on tier - every 90 days on Premium, every six months on Basic.

Physicians are board-certified and described as specializing in hormones and longevity. Consultations are synchronous video visits, not asynchronous questionnaire reviews. There's no auto-renewing contract with a cancellation penalty; membership is month-to-month.

## Pros and cons

Hone's strongest selling point is the lab breadth. If you've ever gotten a standard TRT workup and felt like it was leaving questions unanswered, a 40-plus biomarker panel paired with a physician who focuses on longevity medicine is likely to feel more thorough. Quarterly retesting on Premium keeps the feedback loop tight. FSA and HSA eligibility is useful for men with employer health accounts. The month-to-month structure means you're not locked in.

The main friction is cost complexity. The membership fee is not the total cost - medications run separately, and depending on what you're prescribed, that can add meaningfully to your monthly bill. Without published state coverage information, you also can't confirm eligibility until you're already partway through intake. And without insurance accepted, the full cost lands on you or your benefits account. Compared to platforms where a single monthly fee covers labs, consults, and medications, Hone's pricing requires a little more math to understand what you're actually going to spend.

## Bottom line

Hone Health is a reasonable choice for men who want a genuinely comprehensive hormone and longevity workup rather than a bare-minimum TRT prescription. The tiered structure gives flexibility - the $25 Basic plan works if you want periodic labs and discounted medications without paying for bundled consults; the $149 Premium plan makes sense if you want a physician actively managing your protocol on a quarterly basis. Just go in with clear eyes about the medication costs on top of the membership fee, and confirm state availability before you commit.
