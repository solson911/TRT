## How NRG Clinic works

NRG Clinic uses a synchronous consultation model - meaning your intake is a live conversation with a provider, not an asynchronous message thread. That matters for TRT specifically, because a real-time evaluation gives the clinician a chance to probe your symptoms and history in a way that a static intake form can't. The initial consultation is free, which is a meaningful distinction from many competitors who charge $50-$200 just to get in the door.

Once you've completed your evaluation and been prescribed treatment, medications ship directly to your home. NRG Clinic uses a mixed prescriber model, so depending on your state, your prescription may come from a physician, a nurse practitioner, or a physician assistant. This is fairly common across telehealth TRT providers and doesn't necessarily affect care quality, but it's worth knowing going in.

The clinic currently serves patients in Florida, Texas, Ohio, Nevada, California, Delaware, Kansas, Maryland, New York, Oregon, Utah, and Virginia. If your state isn't on that list, you'll need to look elsewhere.

## Pricing

Public pricing isn't disclosed; you'll get a quote after the intake. That opacity makes it genuinely difficult to comparison-shop before you've invested time in a consultation. NRG Clinic does not accept insurance, so the full cost comes out of pocket. On the positive side, FSA and HSA funds are accepted, which can meaningfully reduce the effective cost for patients who've set aside pre-tax healthcare dollars.

## What's included

NRG Clinic offers three treatment formats: injections, oral medications, and topical preparations such as gels or creams. Having all three on the table is useful - patient preference and clinical fit vary, and not every man wants to manage weekly self-injections. Labs are not included in the program cost, so you'll need to arrange and pay for bloodwork separately. That's a real ongoing expense with TRT, since monitoring testosterone levels, red blood cell counts, and other markers isn't optional - it's the baseline for safe long-term treatment. Budget for it accordingly.

Medications are shipped discreetly to your home, which most patients appreciate for obvious reasons.

## Pros and cons

The free initial consultation stands out as a genuine advantage. It lets you find out whether you're a candidate without any financial commitment upfront, which is a lower-stakes entry point than most telehealth TRT services offer. The synchronous consult model also means you're having an actual conversation with a clinician rather than waiting for an asynchronous response days later. The three-format medication menu - injection, oral, topical - gives more flexibility than programs that only offer one route.

The pricing situation is the most frustrating aspect of NRG Clinic's presentation. When a provider doesn't publish any rate information, it puts the burden on the patient to go through an intake process before knowing whether the program fits their budget. That's a real cost in time, even if the consult itself is free. The cash-pay-only model compounds this - without insurance as a backstop, the full program cost lands directly on the patient every month.

Labs not being included is the other thing worth flagging plainly. Some programs bundle monitoring bloodwork into a monthly fee; NRG Clinic doesn't. If you're comparing monthly costs across providers, NRG Clinic's quoted price won't be the full picture.

## Bottom line

NRG Clinic is a reasonable option for men in its 12-state service area who want a fully online TRT program and are comfortable paying out of pocket. The free initial consult lowers the cost of finding out whether you qualify, and the three medication formats mean you have real options if one delivery method doesn't work for you. The lack of published pricing and excluded labs make it harder to evaluate on cost alone - go in knowing you'll need to ask specifically about what's in and what isn't. If you have an FSA or HSA, that's worth factoring in as a partial offset.
