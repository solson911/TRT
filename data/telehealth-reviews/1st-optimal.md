## What Works and What Doesn't

The all-inclusive pricing model is genuinely straightforward. You pay $159, and everything - labs, supplies, shipping, provider access - is rolled into that number. No surprise charges for syringes or follow-up calls, no separate lab bill. For people who've dealt with fragmented billing from traditional clinics, that clarity has real value.

FSA/HSA acceptance is a practical plus. Not all telehealth TRT providers support it, and if you have funds in one of those accounts, using them here effectively lowers your out-of-pocket cost.

The 10-14 business day fulfillment window is the most concrete drawback. That's two to three calendar weeks from order to delivery in realistic terms. Some competing services get medications out in a week or less. If you're in a hurry to start or coming off an existing protocol, that wait can matter.

State availability is the other question mark. 1st Optimal serves a partial footprint of the US and doesn't publish which states upfront. That's not unusual in this space, but it means you can't rule yourself out without going through intake, which is a mild annoyance.

The company's service mix is worth noting: TRT sits alongside weight loss and peptide programs under the same brand. That's not inherently a problem, but it does mean the platform isn't TRT-specialized. Whether that matters depends on what you want from a provider relationship.

## Bottom Line

1st Optimal is a reasonable option for men in covered states who want a clean, all-inclusive monthly membership for testosterone therapy. The $159 price point is competitive for what's included, the at-home lab process is convenient, and the lack of contracts keeps things flexible. The longer shipping timeline and limited state availability are real constraints - check coverage before signing up, and build in time for that first order to arrive.
