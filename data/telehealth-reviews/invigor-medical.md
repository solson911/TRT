## How Invigor Medical works

Invigor Medical is a nationwide telehealth platform licensed in all 50 states, serving over 35,000 patients across men's health treatments that include hormone optimization, weight loss, peptide therapy, and sexual health. That broad scope is worth understanding upfront: TRT is one product among many here, not the company's singular focus. More specifically, Invigor Medical's hormone offerings center on Enclomiphene rather than direct testosterone replacement - a meaningful distinction that anyone researching traditional TRT protocols should factor in before signing up.

The intake process is fully asynchronous. You complete an online health questionnaire, a licensed physician reviews it, and a treatment plan is issued without a live video or phone call. That means no scheduling friction, but also no real-time conversation with your prescriber. For men who want to ask detailed questions or have a complicated health history, the async model may feel thin.

Once approved, medications ship directly to your door from FDA-registered pharmacies. Invigor Medical offers three delivery formats - injection, oral, and nasal - giving patients some flexibility depending on preference or comfort level with self-injection.

## What's included

Labs are a meaningful gap in Invigor Medical's public information. Whether bloodwork is included in the program fee - or whether patients are expected to obtain it independently - isn't clear. This matters: Enclomiphene therapy and testosterone replacement both require baseline labs and ongoing monitoring to be managed safely. Clarify what's covered before committing.

Medications ship from FDA-registered pharmacies, which provides a reasonable baseline for quality control. That said, some of the compounded medications Invigor Medical prescribes are not FDA-approved as finished drug products. This is common across telehealth platforms that use compounding pharmacies, but it's not a trivial distinction. Ask which of your prescribed medications are compounded and whether commercially manufactured alternatives exist.

The three delivery formats - injectable, oral (primarily Enclomiphene), and nasal testosterone - give patients more options than many single-format platforms. Nasal testosterone gel, in particular, is a less common but legitimate option for men who want to avoid injections.

## Pros and cons

The scale here is a real advantage. Nationwide licensure, 35,000-plus patients, and a 4.7-out-of-5 rating suggest Invigor Medical runs a competent operation and isn't going to leave you hanging on prescription logistics. Compared to smaller telehealth startups with patchy state coverage, that reliability counts for something.

The async model is convenient but limits clinical depth. Hormone therapy isn't a set-it-and-forget-it treatment - dosage needs adjustment, symptoms need tracking, and labs need reviewing over time. A platform that communicates primarily through questionnaires is workable, but it puts more burden on the patient to flag problems and push for attention.

The more significant concern is the Enclomiphene distinction. Enclomiphene is a SERM - it stimulates the body's own testosterone production rather than supplying testosterone directly. It's a legitimate treatment for men with secondary hypogonadism whose hormonal axis is still functional, and it preserves fertility in a way that exogenous testosterone doesn't. But it doesn't work for everyone, and it is not a substitute for testosterone replacement in men with primary hypogonadism or those who need consistently higher testosterone levels. Invigor Medical's website isn't always precise about this, so read the treatment terms carefully before assuming you're getting conventional TRT.

The use of compounded medications adds a layer of uncertainty around consistency and regulatory oversight. It's not unique to Invigor Medical, but it's a factor worth weighing against the cost.

## Bottom line

Invigor Medical is a reasonable option for men interested in Enclomiphene therapy, nasal testosterone, or a broader wellness platform that handles multiple treatments in one place. It covers all 50 states, ships prescriptions reliably, and operates within an accessible price range. If you're specifically looking for injectable testosterone cypionate or enanthate on a straightforward TRT protocol, a dedicated TRT clinic will likely serve you better. Either way, get clear answers on what labs are included and which medications are compounded before starting.
