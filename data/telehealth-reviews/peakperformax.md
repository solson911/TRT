## Pros and Cons

The no-contract structure is a real advantage. TRT works best as a long-term commitment, but starting out, it's reasonable to want a clean exit if the protocol doesn't suit you or if circumstances change. The physician-only prescribing model and the range of delivery formats give this program more clinical depth than the most stripped-down telehealth alternatives, and the $125 starting price is honest for what's on offer.

The gaps are worth naming plainly. State coverage is ambiguous - you may or may not be eligible depending on where you live, and the site doesn't help you figure that out before booking. The lab draw requirement means a trip to a physical location, which is a real inconvenience if you're rural or have a tight schedule. And because the pricing ceiling isn't disclosed, it's hard to budget accurately without completing an intake first. Those aren't disqualifying issues, but they mean the comparison-shopping process requires a phone call rather than a quick scan of a pricing page.

## Bottom Line

PeakPerforMAX is a physician-supervised telehealth TRT option with a reasonable entry price, no contracts, and genuine flexibility in how testosterone is delivered. If you're in their service area and comfortable going to a draw site for labs, it's worth evaluating. Confirm state availability and total pricing before committing time to the intake - those are the two variables the site leaves unanswered.
