## How PeakPerforMAX Works

PeakPerforMAX is a Florida-based telehealth clinic offering testosterone replacement therapy alongside other hormone and wellness treatments. The tagline - "Personalize Wellness Telemedicine Therapies" - is vague, but the program itself is physician-supervised TRT delivered entirely online, with labs handled through established draw sites and medication shipped to your door. It's aimed at men who want professional oversight without commuting to a clinic, and who prefer month-to-month flexibility over a locked-in subscription.

The intake process follows a familiar telehealth path: you schedule a live consultation with a board-certified physician, get bloodwork drawn at a local LabCorp or Quest Diagnostics location, and if TRT is clinically appropriate, your prescription ships to your home. The consultations are synchronous - a real-time call rather than an asynchronous message review - which is the right model for a hormone protocol that requires a physician to actually understand your history and symptoms before writing anything.

One thing to resolve before you schedule: state availability isn't clearly spelled out on their public site. PeakPerforMAX has a Florida address, and telehealth prescribing authority varies significantly by state. If you're outside Florida, confirm they can see you in your state before you invest time in an intake appointment.

## Pricing

PeakPerforMAX advertises a starting price of $125 per month. That's a competitive entry point for a physician-supervised program with labs included. Where pricing goes above that floor isn't disclosed publicly - the actual number you pay will depend on your protocol, which medication form you use, and any add-ons. You'll get a specific quote after your evaluation.

Insurance coverage isn't addressed on their public pages, and there's no mention of FSA or HSA acceptance. Both are worth asking about on your intake call. There are no contracts and no cancellation fees, which is a clean policy for a recurring treatment - verify that in writing once you're enrolled.

## What's Included

Labs are part of the program, which is meaningful. Some lower-priced TRT services advertise a monthly fee and charge separately for bloodwork; labs can easily run $150-$300 without bundling. At PeakPerforMAX, bloodwork appears included, though you'll go to a LabCorp or Quest location rather than using an at-home kit. That requires a bit more logistics than mail-in collection, but it allows for a complete panel rather than a limited fingerstick draw.

Medication ships directly to you once your protocol is established. PeakPerforMAX offers testosterone in four forms: injection, cream, gel, and oral. That's a wider menu than many telehealth-only competitors, which typically default to injections alone. Having cream and gel options matters if injections aren't practical for your routine or if your physician decides a different delivery method is better suited to your levels and response.

## Pros and Cons

The no-contract structure is a real advantage. TRT works best as a long-term commitment, but starting out, it's reasonable to want a clean exit if the protocol doesn't suit you or if circumstances change. The physician-only prescribing model and the range of delivery formats give this program more clinical depth than the most stripped-down telehealth alternatives, and the $125 starting price is honest for what's on offer.

The gaps are worth naming plainly. State coverage is ambiguous - you may or may not be eligible depending on where you live, and the site doesn't help you figure that out before booking. The lab draw requirement means a trip to a physical location, which is a real inconvenience if you're rural or have a tight schedule. And because the pricing ceiling isn't disclosed, it's hard to budget accurately without completing an intake first. Those aren't disqualifying issues, but they mean the comparison-shopping process requires a phone call rather than a quick scan of a pricing page.

## Bottom Line

PeakPerforMAX is a physician-supervised telehealth TRT option with a reasonable entry price, no contracts, and genuine flexibility in how testosterone is delivered. If you're in their service area and comfortable going to a draw site for labs, it's worth evaluating. Confirm state availability and total pricing before committing time to the intake - those are the two variables the site leaves unanswered.
