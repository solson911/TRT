## How Blokes works

Blokes is the men's health side of Joi + Blokes, a dual-brand telehealth platform that serves both men and women under the joiandblokes.com umbrella. The men's offering focuses on hormone optimization - testosterone replacement, enclomiphene, sermorelin, and broader BHRT - with published pricing and health coaching baked into most plans. It operates in all 50 states, which matters in a space where many competitors are still locked out of states like Florida or New York due to prescribing regulations.

The intake process uses synchronous consultations - live video or phone appointments with a clinician, not an async intake form. Once your initial visit is done, at-home lab kits ship directly to you, so there's no separate draw appointment at a local facility. Results come back, a treatment plan is finalized with your clinician, and medications ship to your door from there.

The TRT plan specifically includes ongoing clinician visits as part of the monthly subscription, which means this isn't a prescribe-once-and-disappear model. A health coaching component is also bundled in, covering the lifestyle factors - sleep, nutrition, training - that affect how well hormone therapy actually works. That kind of inclusion is less common than it should be; a lot of platforms just ship testosterone and leave the rest to you.

Treatment delivery options cover injection, topical cream, and nasal gel. The nasal option is worth flagging - it's a less common format that avoids the skin transfer concerns associated with creams and suits men who prefer not to inject. Having all three available means the clinical team can match the method to your situation rather than defaulting to whatever the platform carries most of.

## Pricing

Blokes publishes its prices without making you sit through a sales call first, which is a genuine differentiator in this market.

The core TRT plan runs $149 per month and covers testosterone medication, hormone labs, clinician visits, and coaching - all in. Enclomiphene, an oral option that stimulates the body's own production rather than replacing testosterone externally, is $99 per month. Sermorelin, a growth hormone-releasing peptide aimed at recovery and body composition, runs $199 per month. A general BHRT hormones plan starts at $59 per month for customized hormone support based on your clinical assessment.

Standalone lab panels are available separately: a Complete Hormone Panel covering 56 biomarkers is a one-time $149, an Advanced Panel with 71 biomarkers (adding metabolic and thyroid markers) is $399, and a Comprehensive Panel covering 110 biomarkers is $699.

Insurance is not accepted. FSA/HSA eligibility isn't publicly confirmed - worth asking before you sign up if pre-tax spending matters to your planning.

## What's included

The $149 TRT monthly plan covers testosterone medication, at-home lab kits, clinician consultations, and health coaching. Labs ship to you, you collect the sample at home, and results feed directly into your ongoing care. No separate trip to a draw center.

The coaching component is described as part of the plan but the format and frequency aren't detailed publicly - how often you connect, whether it's one-on-one or group-based, what the scope covers. If coaching is a primary reason you're considering Blokes over a lower-cost competitor, it's a specific question to raise during your intake.

All three delivery formats - injections, creams, nasal gel - are on the menu. Enclomiphene is a meaningful option for men who want to preserve fertility or natural production. Sermorelin sits in a different category from TRT outright - it supports growth hormone release and is typically used for body composition and recovery goals rather than as a primary low-T treatment.

## Pros and cons

The upfront pricing is Blokes' most practical advantage. Knowing the $149 all-in TRT number before any consultation means you can compare it against competitors on a real basis, not an estimated one. The fact that labs and coaching are included - not sold as add-ons - makes that comparison more meaningful.

Nationwide availability is also a genuine plus. All-50-states coverage is still not universal among TRT telehealth providers.

The main drawback is no insurance, which makes this a $99-$199 monthly out-of-pocket commitment depending on your treatment. That's manageable for many, but it's a real number. The other gap is transparency around the clinical infrastructure - no medical director is publicly named on the site, and follow-up frequency and prescriber model details aren't clearly laid out before intake. That's not unusual in this space, but it does mean you're committing to a call before you can fully evaluate the clinical depth behind the platform.

## Bottom line

Blokes offers a straightforward, honestly priced entry point for men in any state who want structured hormone care with labs and coaching included. The range of treatment options, the all-states coverage, and the absence of hidden fees are all things that hold up to scrutiny. The trade-offs - no insurance, limited public detail on the clinical team - are worth noting but aren't disqualifying. Testosterone is a Schedule III controlled substance, and any legitimate platform, including this one, will require a real clinical evaluation before prescribing.
