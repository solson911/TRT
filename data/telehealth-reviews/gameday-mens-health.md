## How Gameday Men's Health works

Gameday Men's Health is a franchise-style men's health clinic network with more than 400 physical locations across the country. Unlike pure telehealth providers that operate entirely online, Gameday runs brick-and-mortar clinics where you walk in, get blood drawn on-site, and speak with a provider face to face. Same-day appointments are available at most locations, which sets it apart from the typical telemedicine flow of waiting days for a video call and then mailing in a blood kit.

The process starts with a free initial consultation. You come in, discuss symptoms and health history, and if labs are warranted, on-site testing returns results in around 15 minutes. From there, a provider puts together a treatment plan. The in-person consult model means less back-and-forth over a patient portal and more of a traditional clinic experience, just with a men's health specialty focus.

Beyond testosterone, Gameday treats a broader range of men's health concerns including weight management, erectile dysfunction, and peptide therapy. The company positions itself as a one-stop shop rather than a TRT-only clinic, which is worth keeping in mind if you're shopping specifically for hormone specialists.

## Pricing

Gameday lists $199 per month for several of its treatment categories, including testosterone cypionate injections, clomid and enclomiphene, GLP-1 medications like semaglutide and tirzepatide, shockwave therapy for ED, and sermorelin peptide injections. That flat rate across different protocols is notable - many competitors price these separately and at different levels. Labs appear to be included in the care process, though whether they're bundled into the monthly fee or handled separately isn't spelled out clearly on public pages.

For treatments outside the listed protocols, public pricing isn't disclosed - you'll get a quote after the intake visit. Whether insurance or FSA/HSA funds can be applied isn't published, so it's worth asking directly at your location.

## What's included

The core TRT offering is injectable testosterone cypionate, the most commonly prescribed protocol. Pellet therapy is also available - a minor in-office procedure every three to six months that eliminates the need for weekly or biweekly self-injections. Oral options round out the delivery methods for men who prefer not to inject.

On the fertility-preservation side, clomid and enclomiphene work by signaling the body to produce more of its own testosterone rather than supplying it exogenously. This matters for men who want to maintain fertility or avoid suppressing natural production entirely.

Shockwave therapy for ED is an in-office acoustic wave treatment aimed at improving blood flow - a legitimate clinical option, though distinct from TRT. Sermorelin, a growth hormone-releasing peptide, sits in a similar lane: adjacent to hormone optimization but not direct testosterone replacement. GLP-1 agonists like semaglutide and tirzepatide round out the weight management side, which has become a near-standard add-on at men's health clinics over the past few years.

## Pros and cons

The in-person model is a genuine advantage for men who prefer not to manage home injection kits alone or who want a provider they can walk in and see. Getting labs back in 15 minutes on-site removes one of the more frustrating friction points in the treatment cycle. Same-day availability across 400-plus locations means geographic coverage is broad, and with more than 50,000 patient reviews averaging 4.9 stars, satisfaction appears consistently high across the network.

The flip side: if you travel frequently, relocate, or live far from a Gameday clinic, the physical model becomes a burden rather than an advantage. The wide service menu - weight loss, hair loss, peptides, ED, TRT - can feel unfocused if you want a provider whose core expertise is hormone optimization rather than a general men's wellness franchise. Pricing transparency is also incomplete; the $199/month figure covers the listed protocols, but the full cost picture - enrollment fees, follow-up visit charges, pellet insertion fees - isn't visible without walking through an intake.

## Bottom line

Gameday Men's Health suits men who want fast on-site labs, face-to-face provider access, and the flexibility to address other health concerns like weight or ED under one roof. It is not a telehealth platform, so if remote-first care is a priority, look elsewhere. For a clinic network of this scale, the flat $199/month pricing on core protocols is straightforward - but go in with specific questions about what's bundled and what's billed separately.
