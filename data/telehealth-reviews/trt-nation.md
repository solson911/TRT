## How TRT Nation Works

The process is entirely remote. After submitting an intake form, you consult with a provider by phone or video. If you're a candidate for treatment, your prescription is written and your medication ships - TRT Nation states most patients receive their order within a week of approval.

One thing to work out before you start: labs. Bloodwork is not included in the monthly subscription. TRT Nation charges a separate $129 fee for labs, and you'll need to visit a local draw site rather than having a kit shipped to your home. That's not unusual in this space, but it does affect your real first-month cost and ongoing monitoring expenses, so build it into your math before signing up. It's also worth confirming during your intake call exactly which lab network they use and how often follow-up bloodwork is required.

State availability isn't disclosed publicly. The clinic notes it doesn't operate in all 50 states, so check whether your state is covered when you create an account.

## Pricing

TRT Nation publishes its prices directly, which is more than a lot of competitors do.

The base **Testosterone** plan runs $99 per month and covers injectable testosterone - the most common TRT protocol, typically administered once or twice a week. At the same $99 price, the **Enclomiphene** plan substitutes an oral medication that stimulates the body's own testosterone production rather than replacing it externally. Enclomiphene is a reasonable choice for men who want to preserve fertility or would rather avoid injections entirely.

The top tier, **TRT + HCG**, costs $180 per month. It pairs injectable testosterone with human chorionic gonadotropin, which helps maintain testicular function and can support fertility during TRT - a combination often requested by men who want a more complete hormonal protocol.

Shipping is free across all plans. There's no public information about whether TRT Nation accepts insurance or works with FSA/HSA accounts, so if that's relevant to your situation, ask during the consultation.

## What's Included

Every plan covers the provider consultation, a prescription if you qualify, the medication itself, and free shipping. The TRT + HCG plan adds HCG to the protocol. What none of the plans include is lab work - that $129 fee sits outside the subscription, meaning your effective first-month cost on the base plan is closer to $228.

Ongoing monitoring labs will add to your out-of-pocket costs beyond month one as well. How frequently those are required depends on your provider's protocol. Before committing, ask how often bloodwork is ordered and what the total annual cost looks like when you factor labs in.

## Pros and Cons

The strongest argument for TRT Nation is straightforward pricing. Three defined tiers at published rates means you can evaluate the cost before ever speaking to anyone - no consultation required just to find out whether you can afford it. Free shipping and a delivery window of under a week are practical advantages worth noting, especially for anyone who has dealt with pharmacy delays on controlled medications.

The enclomiphene option at the same price as the injectable plan is genuinely useful. Many clinics charge a premium for oral alternatives or simply don't offer them. Having that choice without a price penalty is a real differentiator.

The lab structure is the clearest friction point. A $129 add-on is manageable, but it should be disclosed upfront rather than surfaced mid-signup. TRT Nation's limited state coverage is the other variable - you can't know whether it's an issue for you until you try to register. There's also no public information about who leads the medical team or how the prescribers are credentialed, which won't matter to everyone but is something more detail-oriented patients tend to want before sharing their health history.

## Bottom Line

TRT Nation works well for men who want a low-cost, no-frills testosterone program and are comfortable handling labs as a separate step. The $99 base plan is competitive, the enclomiphene option gives oral-preference patients a real path, and the TRT + HCG tier covers men who need a more complete fertility-conscious protocol - all at prices that are clearly posted rather than hidden behind a consultation. If you're in a covered state and the lab structure works for your situation, it's a reasonable place to start.

That said, testosterone is a Schedule III controlled substance. A real clinical evaluation is required before any prescription - not a formality, but an actual medical review of your history, labs, and symptoms.
