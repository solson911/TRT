## How Male Excel works

Male Excel launched in 2019 with a focus on testosterone replacement and thyroid support together - a combination that sets it apart from most telehealth TRT providers, which tend to treat hormones in isolation. The company runs a physician-led model under medical director Peter Fotinos, and it's built for men who want a more complete hormone picture alongside standard TRT rather than a single-symptom fix.

The intake process offers both synchronous and asynchronous consultation options, so you can schedule a video call with a physician or work through an async questionnaire depending on your schedule. There's an initial consultation fee of $99 before treatment begins - that's your first medical evaluation. Once cleared, your prescription ships directly to your door.

Labs are included and handled through a shipped at-home kit, which removes the need to schedule time at a local draw site. That matters in practice: one of the most common reasons men delay or drop off TRT entirely is the friction of getting bloodwork done on someone else's schedule.

## Pricing

Male Excel's pricing runs on two separate charges, and it's worth understanding both before you run the numbers.

First, there's a medical membership fee of $99 per month. This covers unlimited messaging with your care team and ongoing medical support throughout treatment. It's billed separately from the medication itself.

Then there's the medication cost, which depends on your delivery method:

- Oral Triclozene (once-daily tablets with thyroid support): $95/month
- Injectable Testosterone Cypionate (once-daily injections with thyroid tablets): $120/month
- Topical Testosterone Cream (twice-daily cream application with thyroid support): $132/month

Add those together and the real monthly figure is $194, $219, or $231 - not the medication-only numbers you'll see listed first. Stack on the one-time $99 consultation fee at the start and the upfront cost is meaningful before you've confirmed the service is even available in your state.

Insurance isn't accepted. Male Excel does take FSA and HSA funds, which can offset some of the cost if you have access to one of those accounts.

## What's included

Every treatment tier bundles thyroid support alongside testosterone - whether that's the right approach for your specific situation is a conversation for the physician during your evaluation, but having it built in rather than treated as a separate service and a separate bill is convenient. Labs come via shipped kit and are included in the membership. Medication ships to you directly. Unlimited care team messaging is covered under the monthly membership, meaning follow-up questions during the first few months of treatment don't cost extra.

State coverage isn't publicly listed, so you'll need to go through the intake process to confirm Male Excel can prescribe in your state.

## Pros and cons

The 90-day money-back guarantee is the most distinctive thing Male Excel offers. TRT results take weeks to emerge, and men approaching this category for the first time are reasonably skeptical about whether any of it will actually move the needle for them. A real refund window - not a store credit, not a vague satisfaction clause - is genuinely uncommon in this space and worth noting.

The unlimited messaging is useful in a practical sense. The first few months of TRT tend to generate a lot of questions about dosing, side effects, and what to expect, and not having to ration those questions changes the dynamic. The no-contract structure is also a real positive - you can stop anytime without a cancellation fight.

On the downside, the layered pricing is a legitimate complaint. The gap between the headline medication cost and the actual monthly total - once the membership fee is added - isn't obvious upfront, and for some readers it will feel like a bait-and-switch even if both fees are disclosed clearly further down the page. The $99 consultation fee adds to what you're spending before you even know if the service works for you.

The combined monthly cost of $194 to $231 is higher than several competing telehealth TRT providers, particularly those that bundle labs and medical oversight into a single flat fee. For men who are price-sensitive or expecting insurance to absorb part of the cost, this probably isn't the right fit.

## Bottom line

Male Excel is a reasonable pick for men who want testosterone treatment paired with thyroid support, shipped labs, and genuinely unlimited physician access - and who value the security of a 90-day money-back guarantee. The care model is thorough and the no-contract policy keeps the commitment low. Just go in with clear eyes on the full monthly spend: once the membership fee is added, you're looking at $194 to $231 per month, not the lower figures you'll see first. That's before any startup costs.
