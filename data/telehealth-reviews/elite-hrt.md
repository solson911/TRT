## How EliteHRT Works

EliteHRT has been operating since 2013, which puts it in a different category than the crop of telehealth startups that flooded the men's health space after 2019. A decade-plus of practice doesn't automatically mean better care, but it does mean the company has navigated real-world patient management across multiple regulatory cycles - something worth noting when you're evaluating who you'd trust with a controlled substance prescription.

The model is straightforward telehealth. You complete an intake, then connect with a physician through a synchronous consultation - meaning a live conversation, not an asynchronous message thread. That's a meaningful distinction. With a Schedule III controlled substance like testosterone, a real-time clinical conversation gives the prescriber a better read on your situation and gives you a chance to ask actual questions rather than fill out a form and wait.

After the consultation, labs are ordered as part of the program. EliteHRT includes bloodwork in their service rather than billing it separately, though the draw happens at an outside lab location - you're going to a Quest or LabCorp-style draw site, not doing a finger-prick kit at home. That's a minor inconvenience for some patients, but in-person venous draws are the clinical standard for hormone panels, and the results tend to be more reliable than home collection.

If a prescription is appropriate, medication ships directly to your home or office. EliteHRT uses injections as their treatment format - there are no topical creams or pellets listed as options. Injections are the most well-studied delivery method for testosterone replacement and generally allow for more precise dosing adjustments, but they do require self-administration, which isn't for everyone. If needles are a dealbreaker, this particular program isn't a fit.

## Pricing

Public pricing isn't disclosed; you'll get a quote after the intake consultation. EliteHRT doesn't accept insurance or any third-party payment, so the full cost comes out of pocket. Whether FSA or HSA funds can be applied isn't clear from their public materials, so it's worth asking that directly before you commit. The lack of upfront pricing is genuinely frustrating - it's a recurring issue across this category and makes comparison shopping harder than it should be. Go in expecting to have a real conversation about cost before signing up.

## What's Included

The core package covers the physician consultation, a treatment plan, and comprehensive hormone testing. Medication ships to you, which removes the pharmacy trip. The treatment is customized to the individual rather than a fixed protocol, which is appropriate given how much hormone levels and symptoms can vary from patient to patient.

What you won't get here is insurance billing, a walk-in option, or alternative delivery formats beyond injections. The program is fully remote, which suits patients who value convenience and already have some baseline comfort with self-managed health.

## Pros and Cons

The strongest case for EliteHRT is the combination of longevity and the synchronous consult model. Having a live physician conversation at intake - rather than filling out a questionnaire and hoping an algorithm spits out a prescription - reflects a more clinically serious approach. Labs being included rather than billed separately also simplifies the experience and keeps hidden costs down.

The main friction point is pricing opacity. For a company that's been around since 2013, there's no obvious reason not to publish at least a starting price range. The absence of that information pushes the sales process entirely into the consultation, which can feel more like a pitch than a clinical intake. Cash-pay-only is a known constraint in this space - most telehealth TRT programs don't take insurance - but it does mean the full cost lands on you without any offset.

The injection-only treatment format is worth flagging. It's the most common approach in clinical TRT and arguably the most flexible for dialing in dosing over time, but patients who are needle-averse will need to weigh that carefully. There's no pellet or topical option listed, so if you know injections aren't something you're willing to do, shop elsewhere before going through the intake process.

State coverage also isn't listed publicly, which is worth confirming before you invest time in the consultation - prescribing rules vary significantly by state, and not all telehealth TRT programs operate nationally.

## Bottom Line

EliteHRT is a physician-led, synchronous-consult TRT program with over a decade of operating history. It's a reasonable option for men who want a live clinical conversation rather than an automated intake, and who are comfortable with injectable testosterone and out-of-pocket payment. The lack of published pricing makes it harder to evaluate upfront, but if you're already set on the telehealth route and want a provider with some actual longevity behind it, it's worth a consultation to see where the numbers land.
