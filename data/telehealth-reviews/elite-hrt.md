## Pros and Cons

The strongest case for EliteHRT is the combination of longevity and the synchronous consult model. Having a live physician conversation at intake - rather than filling out a questionnaire and hoping an algorithm spits out a prescription - reflects a more clinically serious approach. Labs being included rather than billed separately also simplifies the experience and keeps hidden costs down.

The main friction point is pricing opacity. For a company that's been around since 2013, there's no obvious reason not to publish at least a starting price range. The absence of that information pushes the sales process entirely into the consultation, which can feel more like a pitch than a clinical intake. Cash-pay-only is a known constraint in this space - most telehealth TRT programs don't take insurance - but it does mean the full cost lands on you without any offset.

The injection-only treatment format is worth flagging. It's the most common approach in clinical TRT and arguably the most flexible for dialing in dosing over time, but patients who are needle-averse will need to weigh that carefully. There's no pellet or topical option listed, so if you know injections aren't something you're willing to do, shop elsewhere before going through the intake process.

State coverage also isn't listed publicly, which is worth confirming before you invest time in the consultation - prescribing rules vary significantly by state, and not all telehealth TRT programs operate nationally.

## Bottom Line

EliteHRT is a physician-led, synchronous-consult TRT program with over a decade of operating history. It's a reasonable option for men who want a live clinical conversation rather than an automated intake, and who are comfortable with injectable testosterone and out-of-pocket payment. The lack of published pricing makes it harder to evaluate upfront, but if you're already set on the telehealth route and want a provider with some actual longevity behind it, it's worth a consultation to see where the numbers land.
