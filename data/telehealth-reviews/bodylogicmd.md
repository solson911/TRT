## How BodyLogicMD Works

The intake process starts online - you can request a consultation through their site and connect with a clinician either by video or in person, depending on your location and preference. The company uses a mixed prescriber model, meaning some care is delivered through telemedicine and some through a local affiliate practitioner. That distinction matters here more than it does with most TRT providers, because BodyLogicMD's listed treatment method is pellet therapy. Pellets are small, rice-sized implants placed subcutaneously - typically in the hip or upper buttock - by a clinician during an office visit. They dissolve gradually over three to six months and release testosterone steadily during that time. This is not a self-injection or topical protocol; you will need to appear in person for each insertion procedure.

Lab work is part of the intake process and is included in the program. Testing materials are shipped directly to you, so you can complete bloodwork before your first appointment without hunting down a draw site. After that initial evaluation, clinicians build a personalized plan based on your numbers and symptoms.

## Pricing

Public pricing isn't disclosed; you'll get a quote after the intake. Given the clinical overhead of pellet procedures - which require a practitioner to perform the implant each time - costs are typically higher than self-administered TRT protocols. Don't expect pricing comparable to a monthly injectable subscription service. Budget for both the initial consultation and recurring insertion visits every few months.

## What's Included

Labs are included and materials ship to your door, which removes one common friction point from the early process. Medications - the pellets themselves - are also handled through the clinic's network rather than requiring you to manage a pharmacy relationship on your own. BodyLogicMD also provides a dedicated concierge team for ongoing support, so you have a point of contact between visits if questions come up. Whether that support is clinical or administrative in nature isn't fully spelled out on public pages, so it's worth asking during your intake call.

The company operates in 30 states: Alabama, Arizona, California, Colorado, Connecticut, Florida, Georgia, Idaho, Illinois, Indiana, Louisiana, Massachusetts, Maryland, Michigan, Missouri, North Carolina, New Jersey, Nevada, New York, Ohio, Oregon, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, Virginia, Washington, West Virginia, and Wisconsin. If your state isn't on that list, you'll need to look elsewhere.

## Pros and Cons

BodyLogicMD's strongest selling point is its track record. Two decades in operation means the company has navigated regulatory shifts, built practitioner networks, and presumably refined how it manages ongoing patient care - none of which is guaranteed with newer entrants to the market. The included labs and direct-ship logistics also reduce the administrative burden on the patient side.

The main trade-off is transparency and convenience. Pricing requires a consultation to unlock, which is frustrating if you're early in the comparison process and just want a rough number. The pellet-only treatment model is also a constraint worth thinking through before you commit. It suits patients who want a low-maintenance protocol that doesn't require weekly self-injections or daily topical application, but it does tie you to in-person procedures on a recurring basis. For some people that's a feature; for others it's a dealbreaker.

The site also covers a broad range of services beyond TRT - weight management, integrative medicine, supplements - which can feel like a diluted focus if you want a clinic that specializes exclusively in testosterone optimization. That breadth may reflect the company's integrative medicine philosophy, or it may just be a sign of how the business has grown over 20 years. Either way, the TRT component appears to be a well-established part of the offering rather than an afterthought.

## Bottom Line

BodyLogicMD is a reasonable option for men who want a long-standing, network-backed provider and are specifically open to pellet therapy. It is not the right fit for someone seeking full remote care, self-administered injections, or upfront pricing transparency. If the pellet model appeals and you're in one of the 30 covered states, the intake consultation is a reasonable next step - just go in knowing that the cost discussion happens there, not before.
